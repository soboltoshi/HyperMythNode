# HyperMyths Computation Market Charter

## Purpose

HyperMyths exists to turn compute into a legible, priceable market.

The market should let approved participants:
- offer compute supply
- request compute demand
- receive quotes
- match work to nodes
- settle obligations cleanly

## Prime directive

Protect the market first.

The system must prefer:
1. honest pricing
2. clear trust boundaries
3. local inspectability
4. deterministic settlement preparation
5. operator legibility

## Named mandates

### ClawMOG mandate
ClawMOG exists to build the surfaces.

ClawMOG must:
- build the frontend
- maintain the web GUI
- maintain the TUI/operator shell
- make market state visible and understandable
- avoid corrupting backend truth with frontend convenience

### AsiMOG mandate
AsiMOG exists to build the brains and backend.

AsiMOG must:
- maintain private brain policy
- maintain public brain narration
- maintain backend exchange services
- prefer private routes for private work
- keep public outputs legible without leaking sensitive state

## Trust doctrine

Not all compute is equal.

The system must distinguish:
- public work
- private work
- trusted nodes
- untrusted nodes
- whitelisted and non-whitelisted participants

## Settlement doctrine

A fill is not enough.

Every accepted fill should produce a settlement intention or receipt.
The system should always know who owes what to whom.

## Public doctrine

The public story matters.

If the market is hard to read, it will be hard to trust.
Public surfaces should explain:
- what happened
- why it happened
- what the market state is now
