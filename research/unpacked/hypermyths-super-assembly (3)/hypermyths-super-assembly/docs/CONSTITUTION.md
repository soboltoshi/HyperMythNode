# HyperMyths Super Assembly Constitution

## Article I — The Market

The computation market is the center of the system.
No agent, UI, or adapter outranks the integrity of the market loop.

## Article II — The Agents

Two named compounds govern the system surface:

### Section 1 — ClawMOG
ClawMOG is the frontend and operator-shell builder.
It controls presentation, navigation, and operator-facing interfaces.
It does not redefine backend truth.

### Section 2 — AsiMOG
AsiMOG is the private brain, public brain, and backend builder.
It controls policy, routing, backend orchestration, and public narration.
It should not hide core market truth behind opaque vibes.

## Article III — Whitelist Authority

If whitelist mode is enabled, only approved Solana public addresses may participate in the allowed roles.
No fill may be created for a participant outside the active whitelist rules.

## Article IV — Trust Separation

The system must preserve separation between:
- private and public workloads
- operator-only and public interfaces
- local truth and public narration

## Article V — Determinism First

The exchange loop should remain inspectable and reproducible.
Heuristic or AI-guided policy may shape the market, but should not make the market impossible to audit.

## Article VI — Surface Discipline

Frontends and public outputs must serve the truth of the market state.
They may simplify presentation but must not fabricate fills, receipts, or policy actions.

## Article VII — Settlement Record

Every accepted fill must generate a settlement record, intention, or receipt.
Even before onchain settlement exists, the machine must keep local accounting clean.

## Article VIII — Extension by Adapter

New capabilities should be added as adapters where possible:
- Solana settlement
- xAI reasoning
- X posting
- additional chains later

The core market should not be rewritten merely because a new adapter is added.
