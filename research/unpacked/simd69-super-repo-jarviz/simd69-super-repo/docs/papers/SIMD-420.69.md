# SIMD-420.69
## Observation-Coupled Session-State Optimization in Agentic 42×42×42 Worlds

### Abstract

We propose a system architecture in which execution ordering, world construction, agent learning, and market coordination are unified through a bounded session-state kernel operating over a partially observed three-dimensional world. The system is defined over a deterministic 42×42×42 exploration volume and is governed by a computation market rather than by a purely local game loop or naïve transaction queue. In this model, agents do not merely consume an already-fixed environment. They observe a live world through camera, sensor, or scene-derived inputs; those observations are normalized into canonical session state; and agent actions, trading decisions, training loops, and routing requests alter the future world that subsequent observations will measure. The system therefore exhibits an observation-coupled paradox: discovery and generation are entangled.

At the center of the system is a session-state kernel. The kernel ingests digital activity, validates it, normalizes it into canonical state, orders admissible transitions, enforces time bounds, and manifests results through world changes, interfaces, generated media, agent instructions, or other outputs. The world is governed by a computation market in which significant tasks such as simulation, AI reasoning, routing, analysis, and media generation are represented as jobs with pricing, quotes, fills, receipts, and settlement logic rather than hidden backend magic.

Execution ordering is treated as a constrained batch optimization problem rather than relying only on first-come-first-served sequencing. Candidate state transitions, compute jobs, world mutations, and market actions are collected into bounded execution windows. The system searches for an ordering that best satisfies fairness, execution quality, bounded latency, reduced extractive behavior, and resource discipline. Quantum-assisted or hybrid search may help explore that space, but every candidate plan must still pass deterministic classical verification before acceptance. If optimization fails, the system falls back to a canonical deterministic rule, preserving liveness and replayability.
